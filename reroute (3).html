01 · The Problem

The gap between your goals
and your checkout cart.

We all have savings goals. We all also have Amazon carts. The problem isn't willpower — it's timing. No tool exists at the moment the decision actually happens.

The Pattern

The impulse-purchase loop

  • You find something you want online — it's not urgent, but it's tempting.
  • You justify it in the moment: "I deserve this," "it's on sale," "just this once."
  • You check out. Two days later, you barely remember what you bought.
  • Your savings goal moves no closer. The cycle repeats.
The Real Issue

Savings tools work at the wrong moment

  • Budgeting apps show you what went wrong — after the fact.
  • Bank apps track spending, but don't interrupt it.
  • Nothing lives at the exact moment the decision is made.
  • The checkout button is the decision point. That's where intervention belongs.
"What if the right reminder appeared at exactly the right moment — not to stop you, just to make sure you meant it?"
The ReRoute thesis

03 · The Science

Built on behavioral economics,
not willpower.

ReRoute applies three well-documented behavioral frameworks — not to manipulate, but to help you act in line with your own stated goals.

Framework I
Loss Aversion

People feel losses roughly twice as intensely as equivalent gains. Framing a purchase as "losing ground on your goal" is more motivating than abstract savings advice — and it's accurate.

Framework II
Friction Design

Adding a brief moment of reflection at checkout disrupts the automatic "add to cart → buy" flow. Even small friction reduces impulsive decisions without removing user choice.

Framework III
Commitment Devices

When you name a goal and track progress toward it, you've made a commitment to your future self. ReRoute makes that commitment visible at the moment it counts most.

04 · Research Process

Built with users,
not just for them.

ReRoute is being designed through iterative user research. Every assumption is being tested before a line of production code is written.

Ongoing · User Interviews
Mapping Impulse-Purchase Pain Points

Structured interviews exploring when, where, and why people make purchases they later regret — and what would have made them reconsider.

Testing · UX Prototyping
Intervention Timing & Copy

Iterating on when the nudge appears, what it says, and how it's framed — to maximize opt-in rates without disrupting intentional purchases.

Analysis · Behavioral Econ
Framework Validation

Testing which behavioral economics principles resonate most with real users — loss aversion vs. goal visualization vs. social norms.

Design · Privacy First
Checkout-Only Activation

ReRoute only activates on checkout pages. General browsing is completely private. The product is being designed with privacy constraints as a first-class requirement.

05 · FAQ

Common questions.

ReRoute is early-stage, but here's what we know about how it will work.

Is it actually free?+

Yes. The core extension will be free. Premium features may come later, but the basic nudge-at-checkout experience will always be free to use.

Do you track my browsing?+

No. ReRoute only activates on checkout pages. We have no visibility into your general browsing. Privacy is a first-class design constraint, not an afterthought.

Will it block me from buying things?+

Never. ReRoute shows your goal and a moment of reflection. You always have the option to continue the purchase. There's no blocking, no shame, no lecture — just context.

Which browsers and sites will it work on?+

We're starting with Chrome and Firefox on desktop. Amazon, Target, and Walmart are the initial targets. Mobile and additional sites will follow based on user feedback.
